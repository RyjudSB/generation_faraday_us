The Science

Built on a 200‑year‑old principle of physics. Engineered for the daily abuse of a school year.

Faraday bags work on the principle of electromagnetic shielding discovered by Michael Faraday in 1836. A conductive enclosure prevents radio-frequency signals from entering or leaving. We took that principle, paired it with the same TitanRF fabric trusted by the U.S. Navy and federal forensics teams, and built a bag that holds up to a full bell schedule for years.

Common Questions

Questions & Answers

The questions district administrators, principals, and parents ask most often. Have one we haven't covered? Get in touch.

Why choose Faraday bags over other classroom management options?

Generation Faraday bags are a simple, cost-effective solution that completely block distractions by cutting off all signals. Phones cannot vibrate or ding. Students retain physical possession of their devices, which solves the question of ownership and allows for immediate access in case of emergency. Containment pouches and check-in caddies do not block signal, so the distractions persist.

How do Faraday bags help in classrooms?

Faraday bags give teachers and administrators a tool to maintain a focused learning environment by blocking the signals to students' devices. Attention stays on classwork, free from the constant pull of notifications, texts, and social media.

How exactly do Faraday bags block signals?

The interior of every Generation Faraday bag is lined with TitanRF, a conductive fabric that creates a Faraday cage around the device inside. The cage prevents wireless signals from entering or leaving, effectively silencing the phone for as long as the bag stays closed.

Which signals are blocked?

Cellular (including 5G), Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, GPS, external RFID, and NFC. This comprehensive coverage keeps the device offline and unable to pair with smartwatches, earbuds, or location-tracking services while bagged.

Which devices should go in Faraday bags?

Smartphones, tablets, smartwatches, and small laptops that connect to the internet or use wireless communication are ideal candidates. Keeping these devices offline during instruction protects the focused-learning environment without removing the device from the student's possession.

How do Faraday bags make classroom management easier?

The behavior the policy was designed to produce becomes the default. Devices cannot transmit, so teachers stop policing and start teaching. The day-to-day adjudication that drains teacher energy in off-and-away programs simply does not happen.

Can Faraday bags be used repeatedly?

Yes. Generation Faraday bags are tested to 2,000 open-close cycles, the equivalent of roughly 5.5 school years of daily use. Most competing pouches fail before 200 cycles. The durability white paper is available on request.

In an emergency, can students access their phones?

Yes. In any emergency, the phone can be removed from the bag in seconds, restoring full connectivity. Schools have protocols for emergency access, and teachers can assist as needed.

Can accommodations be made for students with special circumstances?

Yes. Exceptions can be made for students who require phone access for medical, IEP, or other special circumstances. Families coordinate with the school during onboarding to arrange any accommodations.

How do the locking bags work?

The Generation Faraday Max Lock uses a full-metal magnetic clasp that can only be released by an Unlock Dock or handheld key. The mechanism keeps devices secured during instruction and lets staff release the bag quickly at dismissal or in an emergency.

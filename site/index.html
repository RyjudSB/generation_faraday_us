What "Off-and-Away" Actually Looks Like

The policy says phones are off.
The classroom says otherwise.

By the start of the 2026–27 school year, most U.S. states will require bell-to-bell phone-free classrooms. The common answer is off-and-away: silence the phone, put it in a backpack, trust the policy. It fails for three specific reasons, every day, in every classroom it's tried in.

RFIDEnabled

One tag.
Nine possibilities.

Every Generation Faraday bag carries a unique serial number and an embedded RFID tag tucked beneath the logo. That tag is open and compatible with existing RFID infrastructure, which means your district doesn't just get a phone-compliance tool. You get a student identifier that handles attendance, lunch, library, bus access, and emergency accountability from the same chip.
